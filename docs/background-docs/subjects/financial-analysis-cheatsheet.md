# Financial Ratios Cheat Sheet (Chapter 3.2 - Hillier)

---

## I. Short-Term Solvency (Liquidity) Ratios
*Measures a firm's ability to pay its bills over the short run.*

**1. Current Ratio**
* **Formula:** `Current Assets / Current Liabilities`
* **What it measures:** A firm's ability to meet short-term obligations.

**2. Quick (Acid-Test) Ratio**
* **Formula:** `(Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities`
* **What it measures:** A more stringent measure of liquidity that excludes inventory.

**3. Cash Ratio**
* **Formula:** `Cash / Current Liabilities`
* **What it measures:** The firm's ability to pay off current liabilities with only its cash.

---

## II. Long-Term Solvency (Leverage) Ratios
*Measures a firm's long-term ability to meet its obligations and its financial leverage.*

**1. Total Debt Ratio**
* **Formula:** `(Total Assets - Total Equity) / Total Assets`
* **What it measures:** The percentage of the firm's assets financed by debt.

**2. Debt-Equity Ratio**
* **Formula:** `Total Debt / Total Equity`
* **What it measures:** The amount of debt for every unit of equity.

**3. Equity Multiplier**
* **Formula:** `Total Assets / Total Equity`
* **What it measures:** The amount of assets for every unit of equity; 1 + Debt-Equity Ratio.

**4. Times Interest Earned (TIE)**
* **Formula:** `Operating Profit / Interest`
* **What it measures:** How well a firm's operating profit (EBIT) can cover its interest obligations.

**5. Cash Coverage Ratio**
* **Formula:** `(Operating Profit + Non-cash deductions) / Interest`
* **What it measures:** The firm's ability to generate cash (EBITDA) to cover its interest payments.

---

## III. Asset Management (Turnover) Ratios
*Measures how efficiently or intensively a firm uses its assets to generate sales.*

**1. Inventory Turnover**
* **Formula:** `Cost of Goods Sold / Inventory`
* **What it measures:** How many times inventory is sold and restocked in a period.

**2. Days' Sales in Inventory**
* **Formula:** `365 Days / Inventory Turnover`
* **What it measures:** The average number of days inventory is held before being sold.

**3. Receivables Turnover**
* **Formula:** `Sales / Trade Receivables`
* **What it measures:** How quickly the firm collects on its credit sales.

**4. Days' Sales in Receivables (Average Collection Period)**
* **Formula:** `365 Days / Receivables Turnover`
* **What it measures:** The average number of days it takes to collect payment from customers.

**5. Payables Turnover**
* **Formula:** `Credit Purchases / Trade Payables`
* **What it measures:** How quickly the firm pays its suppliers.

**6. Days' Purchases in Payables (Average Payment Period)**
* **Formula:** `365 Days / Payables Turnover`
* **What it measures:** The average number of days the firm takes to pay its suppliers.

**7. NWC Turnover**
* **Formula:** `Sales / Net Working Capital (NWC)`
* **What it measures:** How much "work" (sales) is generated from the firm's net working capital.

**8. PPE Turnover**
* **Formula:** `Sales / Property, Plant and Equipment`
* **What it measures:** The efficiency of fixed assets in generating sales.

**9. Total Asset Turnover**
* **Formula:** `Sales / Total Assets`
* **What it measures:** The overall efficiency of all assets in generating sales.

---

## IV. Profitability Ratios
*Measures how efficiently a firm uses its assets and manages its operations.*

**1. Profit Margin**
* **Formula:** `Net Income / Sales`
* **What it measures:** The percentage of sales that becomes net income.

**2. Return on Assets (ROA)**
* **Formula:** `Net Income / Total Assets`
* **What it measures:** The profit generated per unit of assets.

**3. Return on Equity (ROE)**
* **Formula:** `Net Income / Total Equity`
* **What it measures:** The accounting rate of return for shareholders.

---

## V. Market Value Ratios
*Based on the market price per share; relates market value to accounting figures.*

**1. Price-Earnings (P/E) Ratio**
* **Formula:** `Price per Share / Earnings per Share`
* **What it measures:** How much investors are willing to pay for one unit of the firm's earnings.

**2. PEG Ratio**
* **Formula:** `(P/E Ratio) / (Earnings Growth Rate %)`
* **What it measures:** Adjusts the P/E ratio for the firm's expected earnings growth.

**3. Price-Sales Ratio**
* **Formula:** `Price per Share / Sales per Share`
* **What it measures:** An alternative to the P/E ratio, useful for firms with no earnings.

**4. Market-to-Book Ratio**
* **Formula:** `Market Value per Share / Book Value per Share`
* **What it measures:** Compares the market's valuation of the firm to its historical accounting cost.

**5. Tobin's Q**
* **Formula:** `Market Value of Firm's Assets / Replacement Cost of Firm's Assets`
* **What it measures:** Compares the market value of assets to their replacement cost.
